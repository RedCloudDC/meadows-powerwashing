# Growth Recommendations — Meadows Powerwashing

These are ranked roughly by ROI for a local, seasonal, one-person service business.
The first five cost nothing and will produce 80% of the customers.

---

## Tier 1 — Free, do this first

### 1. Google Business Profile (covered in SETUP_GUIDE.md)
The single biggest lever. Most power washing searches are local — someone types
"power washing near me" or "driveway cleaning Fairfax" and Google shows the Map 3-pack
before it shows regular search results. No profile = invisible.

**Action**: Claim and verify the profile. Upload every before/after photo you take.

### 2. Ask every happy customer for a Google review — the same day
Reviews are the #1 factor in Google's local ranking. Target: 10 reviews in the first
two months. After that, every new review compounds.

**Script to send via text after a job**:
> "Thanks for the business today! If you were happy with the work, would you mind
> leaving a quick Google review? It's huge for a new local business.
> [short link to your Google review page]"

Get the short link from your Google Business Profile → Ask for reviews → copy link.

### 3. Nextdoor
Neighbors are where you'll get your best early customers. They trust each other and
they post asking for recommendations constantly.

**Actions**:
- Create a **Nextdoor for Business** page (free): https://business.nextdoor.com
- Post the before/after photos in 2–3 neighborhood groups where you already live/work.
- When neighbors post "looking for power washing recommendations", reply with a photo
  and a link to the site. Don't spam — reply only to relevant posts.

### 4. Door hangers after every job (highest-conversion flyer tactic)
When a neighbor sees a clean driveway next door, they want one too. Walk 10 houses
on either side of every job with a door hanger that says:

> "We just cleaned your neighbor's driveway at [address].
> Want yours done? New neighbor discount: $25 off. Scan to book."

QR code + phone. Conversion is roughly 3–5x higher than cold flyering.

### 5. Before/after photos on *every* job, both phones
Take 4 shots minimum per job: driveway wide (before + after) and walkway (before + after).
Consistent angle for each pair. These become your Instagram feed, Nextdoor posts, Google
Business uploads, and front of the website.

**Pro tip**: Let a gallon of water sit on the "after" spot for a reflective shine photo.

## Tier 2 — Small budget ($50–200), high return

### 6. Yard signs left at every job
~$8 each at Signs.com. Ask the homeowner if they'll let you leave one for a week.
Offer **$10 off the next job** or **a free mailbox cleaning** in return. Passive
advertising in the exact neighborhood where customers are most likely to convert.

### 7. Car magnets / rear window decal
Truck or SUV rolling to and from every job = moving billboard. ~$30 one-time.

### 8. Seasonal "blast" campaigns (door hangers on HOA streets)
Three times a summer, spend a Saturday walking high-end HOA streets in McLean,
Vienna, Great Falls. Target homes with **visibly dirty driveways** — they're the
easy "yes". Convert rate: 5–15%.

### 9. Simple referral program
**"Refer a neighbor, get $20 off your next wash."**
Print it on receipts. Text it to every customer after the job.
Referrals from existing customers are your cheapest and highest-converting lead.

## Tier 3 — Once cash is flowing

### 10. Google Local Services Ads (LSAs)
Once you have 15+ Google reviews and a verified profile, enable LSAs. You pay per lead
(~$15–40 in NoVA for power washing). The "Google Guaranteed" badge signals trust.
**Only do this after reviews are solid** — low-review profiles get crushed by competitors.

### 11. Meta (Facebook/Instagram) local ads — but only the lookalike kind
Local Facebook/IG ads for a tiny business aren't great for cold traffic, but they're
cheap for **retargeting people who visited the site but didn't book**. Setup is a bit
involved — skip until you're doing $2k+/month and want to squeeze more out.

### 12. Partner with a neighborhood realtor
Realtors desperately need fast "house looking new" services before listing photos.
Offer a 10% realtor discount. Two or three regular realtors = dozens of jobs a year.

## Customer management (keep it dead simple)

You're solo. Don't buy a CRM. Use what you already have.

### Recommended stack (all free)
- **Quote/booking intake** → Formspree → email + SMS (already set up)
- **Job list / schedule** → Apple Reminders or Google Calendar
  - Create a shared calendar "Meadows Jobs". Each job = a calendar event with the
    customer's address in the location (1-tap nav from the calendar).
  - Color-code: green = confirmed, yellow = pending quote, red = needs follow-up.
- **Customer list** → Google Sheets with columns:
  - Date | Name | Phone | Address | Service | Price | Notes | Review left? | Referred by
- **Photos** → iPhone album "Meadows — Jobs" shared with you. One album per year.
  Built-in iOS sharing makes it trivial to send a customer their after photos.
- **Payments** → Venmo, Zelle, or Cash App business accounts. Business accounts are free
  and keep business transactions separate from personal (important come tax time).
- **Expenses & taxes** → Second Sheets tab with columns: Date | Item | Amount | Category.
  Gas, supplies, equipment rental, sign printing all go here.

### The one upgrade worth considering (Jobber)
If Eoin scales past ~30 jobs a month, look at **Jobber** ($29/month). It combines
quoting, scheduling, invoicing, follow-ups, and customer SMS in one app built for
home-service businesses. **Don't buy it day one** — free tools work fine for the first
season.

## Follow-up automation (free, massive impact)

Text customers at three points. This is 80% of repeat business.

### Day 0 — day of job, after finishing
> "Thanks, [name]! Hope you love how it turned out. If you're happy with the work,
> a Google review would mean a lot: [short link]. — Meadows Powerwashing"

### Day 1 — one day later
> "Quick follow-up — anything you want me to come back and touch up? No charge."

### Day 365 — next spring
> "Hey [name], it's Meadows Powerwashing. Your driveway's due for its spring clean.
> Want me to swing by this Saturday?"

The yearly follow-up alone generates a huge chunk of Year 2 revenue. Keep the customer
list up to date and set yearly reminders in Google Calendar.

## Content / social (low priority but free)

One Instagram Reel per week showing a **time-lapse** of a driveway being cleaned.
These routinely go viral — the "oddly satisfying" effect is real. @DirtyDrivewaysUS
built a huge following doing exactly this. Takes 5 minutes to film, 10 to edit, and
occasionally a single reel brings in dozens of leads.

**Easy setup**: Mount the phone on a tripod at one end of the driveway, shoot
time-lapse, clean as usual. Add a trending audio in CapCut.

## Pricing strategy (for when he decides to list prices)

Northern Virginia residential power washing typical ranges:
- Single-car driveway: $120–180
- Two-car driveway: $180–250
- Walkway alone: $75–120
- Driveway + walkway bundle: **$220** (not $300 — bundle discount drives larger tickets)
- Patio (small): $150–200
- Deck (small): $200–300
- Fence (per linear foot): $2–4

Competitive but not cheap. Low prices attract picky, low-margin customers. Price at the
**middle of the market** and compete on responsiveness, photos, and reviews.

**Minimum job**: $125 so you're not driving across the county for a $60 sidewalk.

## Seasonal rhythm

- **March–April**: Spring push. Everyone wants the mildew/algae from winter gone.
  Highest demand of the year.
- **May–June**: Steady, grad parties, graduation, Memorial Day backyard prep.
- **July–August**: Slower (people travel). Good time to do larger custom jobs.
- **September–October**: Fall pre-winter wash. Second peak.
- **November–March**: Closed. Use the website's booking form to collect "waitlist"
  emails for the following spring.

Consider adding a simple "Spring 2027 waitlist" toggle to the booking form in the
off-season.
